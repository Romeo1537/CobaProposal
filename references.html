References and Sources

Primary sources, standards documentation, and guidance materials used to build the compliance knowledge base. All frameworks researched as of May 2026.

ISO/IEC 27001:2022
  • ISO/IEC 27001:2022 — Information security, cybersecurity and privacy protection
    International Organization for Standardization. Published October 2022. The definitive standard document specifying requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an ISMS.
    Standard
  • ISO/IEC 27002:2022 — Information security controls
    International Organization for Standardization. Published February 2022. Reference set of information security controls including implementation guidance. Restructured from 14 domains to 4 themes with 93 controls.
    Standard
  • NCSC: Cyber Security for Small to Medium Organisations
    UK National Cyber Security Centre. Practical guidance on implementing security controls aligned with ISO 27001 for SMEs and startups.
    Guidance
  • IT Governance: ISO 27001 Implementation Guide
    IT Governance Ltd. Comprehensive overview of the 2022 revision changes, clause-by-clause breakdown, and practical implementation advice for UK organisations.
    Guidance
  • Advisera: ISO 27001 Annex A Controls Explained
    Advisera. Detailed walkthrough of all 93 Annex A controls across Organisational, People, Physical, and Technological themes with implementation examples.
    Article
  • BSI: ISO 27001 Certification
    British Standards Institution. Overview of the certification process, audit stages, surveillance cycles, and what auditors expect to see during assessments.
    Guidance
ISO/IEC 42001:2023
  • ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — Artificial intelligence management system
    International Organization for Standardization. Published December 2023. The world's first international standard providing requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and improving an AI management system (AIMS).
    Standard
  • ISO/IEC 23894:2023 — AI Risk Management
    International Organization for Standardization. Guidance on managing risk specifically arising from AI systems, complementing 42001's risk assessment requirements.
    Standard
  • EU AI Act — Regulatory Framework
    European Commission. The EU's risk-based regulatory framework for AI. ISO 42001 alignment supports compliance with EU AI Act requirements, particularly for high-risk AI systems in financial services.
    Regulation
  • BSI: ISO 42001 AI Management System Certification
    British Standards Institution. Overview of the AIMS certification process, relationship to existing ISO management systems, and integration with ISO 27001.
    Guidance
  • NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)
    National Institute of Standards and Technology. US framework for AI risk management. Complementary to ISO 42001 and referenced in cross-jurisdictional compliance approaches.
    Framework
  • ICO: AI and Data Protection Guidance
    UK Information Commissioner's Office. Guidance on AI governance, transparency, fairness, and accountability under UK data protection law. Relevant to 42001's data governance requirements.
    Guidance
  • FCA: Artificial Intelligence in Financial Services
    Financial Conduct Authority. UK financial regulator's position on AI use in regulated firms. Directly relevant to CoBa's banking clients and their expectations of AI governance.
    Regulatory
SOC 2 Type 2
  • AICPA: System and Organization Controls (SOC)
    American Institute of Certified Public Accountants. Authoritative source for SOC reporting frameworks, Trust Service Criteria, and engagement standards.
    Standard
  • AICPA: Trust Services Criteria (2017)
    AICPA. The criteria against which SOC 2 controls are evaluated: Security (Common Criteria), Availability, Confidentiality, Processing Integrity, and Privacy. Includes detailed points of focus for each criterion.
    Criteria
  • Vanta: SOC 2 Compliance Hub
    Vanta. Practical guidance on SOC 2 readiness, evidence collection, control mapping, and the difference between Type 1 and Type 2 reports. Includes timeline expectations.
    Tool/Guide
  • Drata: SOC 2 Evidence Collection Best Practices
    Drata. Detailed guidance on evidence types, automation strategies, population listings, sample selection, and common evidence collection failures that lead to qualified opinions.
    Tool/Guide
  • Schellman: SOC 2 Audit Insights
    Schellman & Company. CPA firm specialising in SOC attestation. Blog covers common audit findings, readiness assessment approaches, and what auditors look for during walkthroughs.
    Article
  • Sprinto: SOC 2 Type 2 Audit Process Explained
    Sprinto. Step-by-step overview of the Type 2 audit process, observation period requirements, and how to maintain control effectiveness throughout the assessment window.
    Article
Cross-Framework and Context
  • ISO: Harmonized Structure for Management System Standards (Annex SL)
    International Organization for Standardization. The common high-level structure (Clauses 4-10) shared by ISO 27001 and ISO 42001, enabling integrated management system implementation.
    Standard
  • CoBa — Official Website
    CoBa. Company website used to understand CoBa's product positioning, compliance module capabilities, and the operational context in which these frameworks apply.
    Company
  • FinTech Magazine — AI in Banking Compliance
    Various articles on AI adoption in banking compliance, regulatory technology trends, and the growing expectation for AI governance frameworks among financial institutions.
    Article
  • Bank of England / PRA: AI and Machine Learning
    Prudential Regulation Authority. UK banking regulator's supervisory expectations for AI/ML use in regulated firms. Context for why CoBa's banking clients require robust AI governance from their vendors.
    Regulatory
  • UK Government: AI Regulation — A Pro-Innovation Approach
    Department for Science, Innovation and Technology. UK's framework-agnostic approach to AI regulation, emphasising existing regulator responsibility. Relevant context for CoBa's UK/EU market positioning.
    Policy
