Days 1-30

Learn the system.

Before building anything, understand how CoBa actually works. Shadow every function, audit every process, learn the product end to end.

AI Workflows

Map current AI usage and identify automation targets

Audit how teams currently use (or do not use) AI across internal processes. Identify the highest-value repetitive tasks: documentation production, data formatting, report generation. Build a prioritised backlog of automation opportunities ranked by time saved and implementation complexity.

Why I can do this
I built this exact audit at the HAC. No centralised system existed. I mapped every operational workflow, identified where time was lost to manual repetition, and built an AI-augmented system in Notion and Claude that now runs how the team operates. Task generation, run sheets, supplier briefs, risk assessments: all structured and repeatable.
Operations and Compliance

Audit the compliance infrastructure Sydnee has built

Review the ISO 27001, ISO 42001, and SOC 2 Type 2 documentation. Understand the evidence structure, the audit cycle, and where maintenance gaps exist. Learn Cyber Essentials Plus and FSQS requirements. Map what is current, what is at risk of drift, and what needs extending for the next audit.

Why I can do this
At the HAC, I produce and maintain H&S risk assessments, event compliance checklists, and regulatory-adjacent supplier documentation across venues from 40 to 2,000 capacity. The discipline of structured evidence for audits is not new. The specific ISO standards are; the rigour is not.
Go-to-Market Support

Understand the Connected Platform and who it serves

Learn how the B2B layer works between CoBa, Lloyds, and the business client. Understand what the sales team needs to close: onboarding flows, product documentation, client-facing materials. Sit with Michele's team and map the gaps between what exists and what they need to sell effectively.

Why I can do this
I create materials that help other people do their jobs. At the HAC, run sheets and supplier briefing packs are built so that someone who was not in the planning meeting can pick them up and execute. For the family catering business, I built the website and client-facing systems so the business could function without everything running through me.

Days 30-60

Build the framework.

Ship the first working systems. Document core processes in AI-trainable formats, update compliance evidence, and start building what the sales team needs.

AI Workflows

Ship the first AI-powered workflow into internal use

Design, test, and deploy at least one repeatable AI workflow: prompt library, agent-assisted documentation, or automated report generation. Train the team to use it independently. Measure adoption and time saved. Build it as a system, not a personal shortcut.

Why I can do this
I maintain a full Claude Code workflow that I use daily for planning, automation, and document production. I also built a job search pipeline that scores roles across six weighted criteria, auto-generates tailored CVs and cover letters, produces structured company research briefs, and tracks everything in a live dashboard. The entire system runs on Claude. This is not theory. It is how I work.
Operations and Compliance

Bring compliance documentation current

Update ISO policy and procedure registers to reflect what CoBa actually does today. Prepare the evidence base for the next audit cycle. Document the first set of core business workflows in structured, reusable formats that can be maintained without starting from scratch each time.

Why I can do this
The JD says this role is about mapping and documenting business functions to create reusable frameworks. That is exactly what I did at the HAC, in an environment with no precedent and no brief. I documented processes I had never seen before by shadowing, mapping inputs, outputs, and handoffs, and codifying the reality rather than the theory.
Go-to-Market Support

Build the first GTM knowledge base materials

Produce the first set of client onboarding documentation, product guides, and sales enablement materials for the Connected Platform. Structure them so the sales team can use them independently and so they can be updated as the product evolves. Quality standard: every document should be clear enough that someone who was not in the room can act on it.

Why I can do this
At the HAC, I produce supplier briefing packs and client communications that the team uses to deliver events independently. For the catering business, I manage the website and create materials that support client acquisition and event delivery. The core skill is the same: turning what one person knows into something a team can use.
