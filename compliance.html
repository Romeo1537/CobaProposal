ISO/IEC 27001:2022

Information Security Management System

The international standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an information security management system (ISMS). The foundation of CoBa's security posture.

What It Is

A risk-based framework for protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information. Not a checklist. An ongoing management system that requires continuous assessment, treatment, and improvement of security risks.

Why CoBa Needs It

CoBa handles sensitive financial data between businesses and banks. ISO 27001 certification signals to banking partners that CoBa meets international security expectations. It is often a procurement prerequisite for regulated financial institutions.

Certification Cycle

Initial certification audit followed by annual surveillance audits and a full recertification every three years. Requires demonstrable evidence of ongoing operation, not just documentation written at certification time.

These clauses define the management system itself. They are non-negotiable requirements.

Evidence Documentation

Auditors expect to see living evidence that the system operates as documented. Key evidence types:

Policies and Procedures

  • Information Security Policy (top-level)
  • Risk Assessment Methodology
  • Statement of Applicability (SoA)
  • Access Control Policy
  • Acceptable Use Policy
  • Incident Response Procedure

Operational Records

  • Risk register with treatment decisions
  • Internal audit reports (Clause 9.2)
  • Management review minutes (Clause 9.3)
  • Corrective action records
  • Training and competence records
  • Change management logs
ISO/IEC 42001:2023

AI Management System

The world's first international standard for responsible AI governance. Published December 2023. Directly relevant to CoBa's AI-powered compliance module.

What It Is

A framework for organisations that provide or use AI systems to manage AI-specific risks: bias, transparency, safety, accountability. Follows the same Clause 4-10 high-level structure as ISO 27001, making integration straightforward.

Why CoBa Needs It

CoBa's product includes AI-driven compliance and document processing capabilities. Banking partners operating under financial regulation will increasingly expect AI governance certification. Early adoption signals maturity and builds trust with regulated buyers.

Relationship to 27001

Built on the same Annex SL management system structure. Organisations already certified to ISO 27001 can integrate 42001 into their existing ISMS rather than building a parallel system from scratch. Many controls overlap.

Core Requirements

AI Policy and Objectives

Establish a formal AI policy endorsed by top management. Define measurable objectives for responsible AI use aligned with organisational values and regulatory obligations.

AI Risk Assessment

Identify and assess risks specific to AI systems: data quality, model drift, adversarial attacks, unintended outputs. Map risks to the AI lifecycle (design, development, deployment, retirement).

AI Impact Assessment

Evaluate the potential effects of AI systems on individuals, groups, and society. Consider bias, discrimination, privacy, safety, and environmental impact. Document mitigations.

Human Oversight

Define where human intervention is required in AI decision-making. Establish clear escalation paths, override capabilities, and accountability structures. No fully autonomous decisions in high-risk contexts.

Transparency and Explainability

Document how AI systems reach decisions. Provide explanations appropriate to the audience (technical for developers, plain language for affected parties). Maintain model documentation.

Data Governance

Manage training data quality, provenance, and representativeness. Address bias in datasets. Ensure data handling meets privacy requirements throughout the AI lifecycle.

Third-Party AI Oversight

Where AI components are sourced from third parties (e.g. LLM providers), apply equivalent governance. Assess supplier AI practices, document shared responsibility boundaries, monitor ongoing performance.

Ongoing Maintenance

ISO 42001 is not a one-time certification. These activities must run continuously:

Periodic Activities

  • Model performance monitoring and drift detection
  • Bias and fairness audits on production outputs
  • AI risk register review (quarterly minimum)
  • Internal AIMS audits (Clause 9.2)
  • Management review of AI objectives (Clause 9.3)
  • Incident analysis for AI-related failures or near-misses

Trigger-Based Activities

  • Re-assessment when models are retrained or updated
  • Impact assessment when deploying AI to new use cases
  • Supplier review when changing AI providers
  • Regulatory change assessment (EU AI Act, FCA guidance)
  • Corrective action for identified nonconformities
  • Stakeholder communication when AI decisions are contested
SOC 2 Type 2

Service Organisation Controls

An attestation engagement where an independent CPA firm evaluates the design and operating effectiveness of controls over a sustained period. Not a certification. An opinion.

Type 1 vs Type 2

Type 1 evaluates control design at a single point in time. Type 2 evaluates both design and operating effectiveness over a period (typically 3-12 months). Type 2 is significantly more rigorous and is what enterprise buyers expect.

Why CoBa Needs It

SOC 2 is the standard due diligence expectation for SaaS vendors handling customer data. Banking and enterprise clients will request a SOC 2 Type 2 report before procurement. It validates that CoBa's controls operate effectively over time, not just on paper.

The Report

Issued by the CPA firm. Contains: management's assertion, the auditor's opinion, system description, control objectives, tests performed, and results. Shared with prospective clients under NDA. A qualified opinion (exceptions noted) can still pass if exceptions are minor.

Five Trust Service Criteria

Security is mandatory. The remaining four are selected based on relevance to the service.

CriteriaFocusCoBa Relevance
Security (CC) Protection against unauthorised access Mandatory. Core platform access controls, encryption, network security.
Availability (A) System uptime and performance commitments High relevance. Banks expect guaranteed uptime for compliance workflows.
Confidentiality (C) Protection of confidential information High relevance. CoBa processes sensitive business and financial data.
Processing Integrity (PI) System processing is complete, accurate, timely Relevant. AI-processed compliance outputs must be accurate and auditable.
Privacy (P) Personal information handling Moderate. Depends on whether CoBa processes PII directly.

Audit Preparation Requirements

What must be in place before the observation period begins:

Control Environment

  • Formal policies for each applicable Trust Service Criteria
  • Defined control objectives with mapped procedures
  • Assigned control owners for every process
  • Automated evidence collection where possible
  • Exception and incident tracking system

Evidence Standards

  • Population listings (complete records, not samples)
  • Timestamped proof of control execution
  • Screenshots, system logs, or automated exports
  • Remediation evidence for any identified gaps
  • Consistent execution across the full observation period

Readiness Activities

  • Gap assessment against Trust Service Criteria
  • Remediation of identified control deficiencies
  • Staff training on control execution and evidence
  • Dry-run audit with sample testing
  • Selection of CPA firm and scoping engagement

CoBa Application

What This Means for the Role

How these frameworks connect to the AI Operations Analyst position and CoBa's compliance infrastructure.

01 ISO 27001 Evidence Management

The role involves maintaining living evidence that CoBa's ISMS operates as documented. This means keeping the risk register current, ensuring internal audit schedules are met, preparing management review packs, and maintaining the Statement of Applicability as controls evolve. Evidence must be audit-ready at all times, not assembled retrospectively before surveillance visits.

02 ISO 42001 Maintenance

CoBa's AI module requires ongoing governance under 42001. This includes monitoring model performance, conducting periodic bias audits, maintaining AI impact assessments as use cases expand, managing third-party AI supplier relationships, and ensuring human oversight mechanisms remain effective. The standard is new (2023), so establishing robust processes now creates competitive advantage.

03 SOC 2 Audit Preparation

Preparing for SOC 2 Type 2 involves building the control environment, automating evidence collection, training staff on consistent execution, and running gap assessments. The goal is to reach a position where the observation period can begin with confidence that controls will operate effectively throughout. This is operational work: process design, documentation, and coordination across engineering and compliance functions.

04 Integration Opportunity

All three frameworks share common structural elements. A unified compliance operating system that manages policies, risks, evidence, and audit schedules across all three frameworks reduces duplication and ensures consistency. This is where process design and operational efficiency meet compliance. The ability to design and maintain such a system is central to the value proposition of this role.
